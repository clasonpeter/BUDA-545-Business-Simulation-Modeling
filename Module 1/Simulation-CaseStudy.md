# Case Study

## Overview

| # | Section | Details |
|---|---------|---------|
| a | **Goal** | Build a simulation-based digital twin for two NHS hospitals to investigate what-if scenarios for improving hospital operations, patient experience, and resource allocation. |
| b | **Why Simulation** | Hospitals had complex patient pathways and limited resources, making it difficult to predict how changes in one department would affect the rest of the system without a safe testing environment. |
| c | **Challenges** | Managing competition for beds between emergency and planned patients, handling specialty mismatches when wards were full, and tracking priority-based queuing across 75 hospital units across two facilities. |
| d | **Outcomes** | Enabled managers to run capacity stress tests, plan new wards, track bed utilization, monitor queue times, and measure patient time spent in each location — all without disrupting real hospital operations. |
| e | **Target Audience** | Hospital administrators and NHS managers improving patient flow and resource planning through simulation, and healthcare organizations considering digital twin technology for operational decision making. |
